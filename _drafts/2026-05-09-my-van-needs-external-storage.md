Ry,
my 2022 Thor Rize,
(now [for sale](/thor-rize-campervan-for-sale))
has plenty
of internal storage.

## Factory-installed storage

There are
three large cabinets
on the driver side,
along with
shelving and drawers
behind them.
On the passenger side
there is
another cabinet, more drawers,
and a microwave
that is great
for storing
plates
and paper towels.
In the bathroom,
there is
a medicine cabinet,
large drawers
and a closet,
along with
a large shelf,
and some netting
accessible
from the back.
And that's
in addition to
the "pizza oven" shelf
above the cab
and two
large glove compartments
on the passenger side.

But it's still
difficult
to find space
inside the van
for some things.

## Stinky slinky

Thor installs
a grey water hose
compartment
underneath
the seating area
that is accessible
from outside
and keeps
grey water
from dripping
on anything inside.

I removed
the compartment
to make space
for my lithium battery
upgrade.

Even if I hadn't,
I also upgraded
to a better quality
grey water hose
that wouldn't fit
in that compartment.

## Snowboard

None of
the numerous storage areas
are big enough
for a snowboard.

My snowboard fit
pretty well
on the floor
behind the seats.
But that location
created a trip hazard
when entering
or exiting
the cab.

It also required
making sure
there was
no snow or slush
on the board
before putting
it away,
otherwise
the melting snow
would drip
onto the floor.

My campervan needs
external storage.

This is
one of
the reasons
I am
[selling my campervan](/thor-rize-campervan-for-sale).
It doesn't meet
all of
[my needs](/my-van-needs).
For example,
I need
[four-wheel-drive](/my-van-needs-four-wheel-drive).
