Ry,
my 2022 Thor Rize,
(now [for sale](/thor-rize-campervan-for-sale))
came with
an enclosed
wet bath.
That was one
of its
biggest selling points,
a place where
I could stand and take
a hot shower
after a long day
of snowboarding
or before
going out to be
in and amongst
family, friends, and the public.
Unfortunately,
this didn't work out
as well
as I had hoped.

You know
when showering
in a campervan
without hookups
you are going
to be making
some sacrifices.

Ry has
an 18-gallon
fresh water tank
and a 20-gallon
grey water tank.
In addition,
the hot water tank
is only
2.6 gallons.
A typical showerhead
sprays water
at the rate
of about
1 gallon per minute.

If you want
water for
drinking and cooking
and want
to go
several days without
dumping the grey water and
filling the fresh water,
you can't take showers
the same way
you probably do
in a stationary home.

## Navy showers

Navy showers are simple.

1. Turn on the water.
2. Get wet.
3. Turn off the water.
4. Lather up.
5. Turn on the water.
6. Rinse off.
7. Do not repeat.

It's effective.
It uses
very little water.
But it takes away
a luxury
that most
of us
are used to
and deeply miss.
You can't linger.
You can't
take your time
and enjoy
the warm water
flowing over you.

## Recirculating shower

Long lingering showers
are simply
not possible
in a campervan
unless you have hookups
or you recycle
the water.

It turns out
that recycling
the water
in your shower
is viable.
Several DIYers
have built
working recirculating showers.
One couple
tested their water
after reusing
the same
2 gallons
for a week
and it
came back safe.

Multiple retailers
in the US
are working
on recirculating systems
for campervans.

A recirculating shower adds
quite a bit
of complexity.
The filters need
to be cleaned
every week
or so.
And petroleum-based
soaps and shampoos
must be avoided.
But this seems
well worth it
to be able
to take
hour long showers
in your van.

This is
one of
the reasons
I am
[selling my campervan](/thor-rize-campervan-for-sale).
It doesn't meet
all of
[my needs](/my-van-needs).
For example,
my van needs
[to be maintainable](/my-van-needs-to-be-maintainable).
