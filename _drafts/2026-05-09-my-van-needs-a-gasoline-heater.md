Ry,
my [TK year] Thor Rize,
came with
a built-in
five gallon [TK check volume]
propane tank.
Propane
is used
to power
the heater,
the stove,
and the generator.
The chassis gasoline tank
fueled the vehicle engine.
Coach batteries powered
everything else.

Every
additional energy source
adds mechanical
and operational complexity.
A propane tanks needs
to be filled.
Levels must
be monitored.
Pipes, tanks, and connectors
can fail.

## All Electric

In my dreams,
my campervan is
all-electric.
It is
clean, quiet, and environmentally friendly.

But the reality
right now
is that batteries
are expensive,
slow to charge,
require infrastructure
that isn't universal,
and do not
have sufficient capacity
for an all-electric campervan.

Ram actually sells
an [electric Promaster](https://www.ramtrucks.com/electric/ram-promaster-ev.html),
but the 164-mile range
is insufficient
for camping.
Even if it had
twice that capacity,
it's not enough
to keep you warm
when camping
near ski areas.[TK link to ski lodge]

## Petrocarbon

Instead of adding
an additional fuel tank,
campervans can run
heaters and water heaters
using gasoline or diesel
from the chassis fuel tank.

Without a propane tank,
all the fuel
that the van needs,
can be acquired
quickly, easily,
and in far more places.

Because of
how they are installed
these systems
cannot use
the last quarter tank,
preventing you
from being stranded
because you ran
the heater too long.

You cannot use
gasoline or diesel
directly in a stove
so not having propane
means switching
to an induction cooktop.
That's probably better
than burning propane
in your tiny living space
anyway.
